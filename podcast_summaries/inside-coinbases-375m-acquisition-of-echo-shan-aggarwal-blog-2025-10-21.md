# Inside Coinbase's $375M Acquisition of Echo: Building the Capital Markets Stack On-Chain

**Source:** Empire Podcast
**Guest:** Shan Aggarwal, Chief Business Officer at Coinbase
**Published:** October 2025
**Duration:** 1 hour, 1 minute

---

## Introduction

In a revealing conversation on the Empire podcast, Coinbase's Chief Business Officer Shan Aggarwal pulls back the curtain on the exchange's $375 million acquisition of Echo, a platform enabling compliant on-chain fundraising. But this isn't just another M&A announcement—it's a window into Coinbase's ambitious vision to rebuild capital markets from the ground up using blockchain infrastructure.

Aggarwal, who has led Coinbase's corporate development efforts for nearly eight years and overseen 40 acquisitions, shares the strategic thinking behind moving from being primarily a secondary trading marketplace to becoming the complete financial stack for crypto companies and beyond. The conversation reveals how Coinbase is positioning itself at the intersection of traditional finance and crypto, with Echo as a critical piece of infrastructure to bring capital formation on-chain.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. **The Build vs. Buy Framework: Why Coinbase Chose to Acquire Echo**

Aggarwal outlines Coinbase's disciplined approach to M&A decisions, which always starts with product strategy and customer needs over a multi-year horizon. The question isn't whether they *could* build something internally, but whether acquisition offers a faster, higher-quality path to market.

> "We don't have any hubris about it. We're like actually isn't so much better for us to just acquire that company, bring them internally to Coinbase, empower that leader to actually drive greater impact from a platform like Coinbase that has global scale at this point."

For Echo, the decision was clear. Echo had already established itself as the leader in on-chain capital formation with over $200 million raised across 300 investments, including high-profile projects like Plasma and Mega ETH. Rather than starting from scratch, Coinbase could immediately access proven technology, an experienced team led by founder Kobe, and existing market traction. The acquisition brings an end-to-end solution for compliant fundraising directly to Coinbase's platform, allowing builders to raise capital from their most engaged users rather than just institutional VCs.

The relationship began organically—Echo was built on Base (Coinbase's Layer 2), and the companies had already launched an Echo fund for the Base ecosystem earlier in 2024. As they worked together, both sides realized there was a much bigger opportunity to expand beyond just the Base ecosystem and make on-chain capital formation available to the entire crypto industry.

### 2. **From Trading Platform to Complete Capital Markets Stack**

The Echo acquisition represents a strategic shift for Coinbase from being primarily a secondary trading marketplace to offering the complete financial infrastructure for crypto companies throughout their lifecycle.

When combined with Coinbase's earlier acquisition of Liquify (a cap table management platform), the company now controls the entire capital markets stack:

- **Company formation and cap table management** (Liquify)
- **Compliant fundraising and token sales** (Echo)
- **Secondary trading** (Coinbase Exchange)
- **Custody and institutional services** (Coinbase Prime)

> "Our vision or our mission at Coinbase has always been to create more economic freedom in the world. And we believe that crypto and blockchain protocols allow you to have more open and accessible financial services. And so Echo really gives us the components to actually do this compliant fundraising on chain in a way that we think better connects capital or investors with investment opportunities."

The vision extends well beyond crypto-native projects. Aggarwal sees a future where tokenization bridges traditional finance and crypto, with the same infrastructure supporting both crypto tokens and tokenized traditional assets. In this world, a user could open their Coinbase account and not only trade Bitcoin and Ethereum but also participate in Series A investments, public token sales via Sonar, and eventually even trade tokenized equities—all from the same platform.

### 3. **The Art of M&A: Missionaries Over Mercenaries**

Perhaps most illuminating is Aggarwal's philosophy on successful integrations. Three current Coinbase executives came through acquisitions: Greg Tusar (founder of Togo, now leading institutional business), Rob Widoff (Unit 410, now leading engineering), and Jesse Pollock (now leading Base).

The secret? Coinbase prioritizes cultural fit and long-term mission alignment over everything else.

> "We really want missionaries not mercenaries, right? And I think like in M&A, especially traditional M&A, a lot of it is mostly mercenary, right? It's like how much are you going to pay me and like how long do I have to stay and what's my earnout and like that's it."

Aggarwal actively avoids complex earnout structures with multiple performance milestones, arguing they create misalignment of incentives. When a team is narrowly focused on hitting specific metrics that drive their earnout, they lose sight of the bigger picture and what's best for the combined company. Instead, Coinbase prefers to get everyone on the same team working toward the same goals.

His rule of thumb: earnouts only make sense when the acquired team has true autonomy to control the outcome. Otherwise, you end up with arguments about whether failures were due to execution or decisions made by the parent company.

The deal structure varies case-by-case—there's no rigid formula. Echo's acquisition will be a mix of cash and Coinbase stock, with the final value between $350-400 million depending on the share price. But the focus is always on creating genuine alignment rather than gaming incentive structures.

### 4. **Prediction Markets: The Next Mass Market On-Ramp to Crypto**

Beyond the Echo acquisition, Aggarwal revealed that Coinbase is aggressively moving into prediction markets, with recent investments in both Polymarket and Kalshi. More significantly, Coinbase plans to integrate prediction markets directly into its platform—potentially offering sports betting within the next 12 months.

The strategic rationale is compelling: prediction markets can introduce mainstream users to crypto who might have viewed it as "fringe" but feel comfortable betting on an NFL game. It's a natural bridge to a "much more mass market audience than traditional crypto trading."

> "There's this move towards like over financialization of everyday things where people want to have skin in the game whether it's you know if they're attending a game on the weekends or if it's for an election or whatever it is."

Looking at the top 20 trades on Polymarket in a given week—all sports bets during football season—it's clear where user demand is concentrated. Coinbase sees an opportunity to capture this demand while simultaneously onboarding users to crypto infrastructure.

Interestingly, Aggarwal sees room for multiple winners in the prediction markets space. Rather than one generalist platform, he envisions specialization similar to derivatives exchanges, with different platforms optimizing for different verticals: real-time events, sports with complex parlays, politics, or permissionless user-generated markets.

## Notable Quotes

**On Coinbase's attempt to tokenize its own stock during its 2021 IPO:**
> "A lot of people don't know is actually we tried to tokenize Coinbase when we went public and we had sort of a dual track process that we were working on at that time, but we just ran into a brick wall on the regulatory side. So we ended up dropping it then so that we could get out."

**On what makes companies attractive acquisition targets:**
> "The best acquisitions that we've had have been actually situations where we've taken something that someone has built and we've combined it with things that Coinbase has and made it into something new."

**On M&A outreach:**
> "Companies are bought not sold... I'd say probably like 75-25, 75% outbound, 25% inbound. It comes back to we have more visibility into the direction that we want to take Coinbase."

**On the future of tokenized equities:**
> "I'm very confident that there will be a market for that. It's just right now once you have the on-chain security there's not really a lot that you can do with it because it's unclear like who can you transfer it to, where can you trade it, can you have lending markets on top of it."

## Actionable Takeaways

**For Crypto Founders:**
1. **Build relationships before you need them** - Coinbase has acquired 40 companies, mostly through outbound approaches. Being on their radar through Coinbase Ventures investments or business development relationships creates pathways for future acquisition discussions.

2. **Focus on what you'll build together, not what you've built** - Aggarwal emphasizes that the best pitches focus on future opportunities unlocked by combining forces, not historical achievements. Think about what becomes possible at Coinbase's scale that isn't possible standalone.

3. **Avoid investment bankers if possible** - Coinbase strongly prefers direct negotiations with founders. Only a handful of their 40 acquisitions involved sell-side bankers. Direct relationships enable smoother deals and better cultural assessment.

4. **Cultural fit matters more than financial engineering** - Complex earnout structures often create more problems than they solve. Demonstrate genuine mission alignment and long-term commitment to the space rather than focusing purely on deal terms.

**For Investors:**
1. **Pay attention to Coinbase Ventures' portfolio** - The ventures team (500+ investments) serves as a leading indicator of where the industry is headed and which companies might become acquisition targets. Though not all acquisitions come from the portfolio, there's meaningful overlap.

2. **The capital markets stack is still being assembled** - While Coinbase has made major moves with Liquify and Echo, gaps remain in the on-chain capital markets infrastructure. Companies building complementary pieces (particularly around compliance, identity, and regulatory infrastructure) are strategically valuable.

3. **Tokenization is the bridge between TradFi and crypto** - Aggarwal sees convergence coming between traditional finance and crypto, with tokenization as the bridge. Companies enabling this transition (like Superstate) are positioned at a major inflection point.

**For the Crypto Industry:**
1. **Prepare for on-chain capital formation** - The infrastructure is being built now for companies to raise capital entirely on-chain in a compliant way. This will fundamentally change how crypto companies (and eventually all companies) access capital.

2. **Specialization over generalization in emerging categories** - As prediction markets and other new categories emerge, Aggarwal expects specialization similar to derivatives exchanges rather than winner-take-all dynamics. There's room for multiple platforms with different strengths.

3. **Crypto cycles are moderating but not disappearing** - While Aggarwal believes another bear market will eventually come, he expects it to be less severe than 2018 or 2022 thanks to real use cases (like stablecoins) that are less correlated with crypto asset volatility.

## Conclusion

The Echo acquisition isn't just about adding another product to Coinbase's suite—it's about positioning the company at the foundation of a rebuilt financial system. By controlling the entire capital markets stack from company formation through fundraising to trading, Coinbase is betting that the future of finance runs on-chain and that they can provide the infrastructure to make it happen.

What's particularly striking is how Aggarwal frames the opportunity: not as crypto competing with traditional finance, but as convergence through tokenization. The same infrastructure built for crypto tokens can support tokenized equities, real-world assets, and eventually any financial instrument. The regulatory challenges are significant, but the technical pieces are falling into place.

For crypto founders, the message is clear: Coinbase is building the platform to support the entire company lifecycle, from cap table management through public token sales to secondary trading. For the industry, it signals that on-chain capital formation is moving from experiment to infrastructure—and the company with the most complete stack may win the next era of finance.

The question isn't whether capital markets will move on-chain, but how quickly it will happen and who will provide the rails. Coinbase is placing a $375 million bet that with Echo, they've secured a critical piece of that future.
