# Plasma TVL Growth, Prediction Market Momentum, and Hypurrr NFTs

**Source:** 0xResearch Podcast
**Episode Length:** 58 minutes
**Published:** October 2025
**Hosts:** Bakio & Ryan

---

## Introduction

The crypto narrative landscape is shifting rapidly. While memecoins dominated early 2025, new catalysts are emerging across DeFi infrastructure, prediction markets, and NFT airdrops. In this livestream, the 0xResearch team dissects Plasma's explosive $5B TVL launch, the surprising growth of cultural betting markets on Polymarket, and a costly airdrop decision that left some users missing out on $70,000. The conversation reveals how market attention cycles between narratives—and why positioning early in these rotations matters.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Plasma's Incentive-Driven Launch Sets New Standards for Stablecoin Chains

Plasma achieved over $5 billion in TVL within its first week through aggressive liquidity mining—emitting approximately $600 million annually in incentives. This represents a new playbook for stablecoin-focused chains: massive upfront capital deployment to attract "comfortable capital" sitting idle on Ethereum mainnet.

**The Strategic Context:**
Plasma isn't competing on revenue—it's competing on distribution. As one host noted, "there is no other coin apart from USDE that is a pure bet on stablecoins... Plasma has the additional benefit of also being Tether beta." The chain secured Binance integration for Plasma Earn and launched Plasma One to onboard users from centralized exchanges onto their native app.

**The Risk:**
The biggest challenge ahead is converting short-term incentive farmers into sticky users. The team referenced historical patterns: "A lot of these high incentive amounts are being paid out in the native token and usually there's some cyclicality to that type of stuff." Success will depend on whether Plasma can maintain momentum when emissions inevitably decrease.

> "This is the result of very heavy incentivization. There is a lot of very comfortable capital out in the EVM, most of it parked in Ethereum mainnet... If you have capital laying around, barring smart contract risk, this is pretty attractive stuff."

### 2. Prediction Markets Are Capturing Non-Crypto Consumer Attention

Polymarket's cultural betting markets are experiencing consistent growth, with volumes in pop culture bets (celebrity tweets, content creator metrics, entertainment outcomes) climbing steadily over recent months. This marks a departure from the platform's election-focused origins.

**Why It Matters:**
Unlike traditional sports betting platforms, prediction markets enable bets on unprecedented categories—from Elon Musk's tweet count to Mr. Beast video views. This creates a new consumer vertical that feels less like CT-native trading and more like mainstream entertainment betting.

The hosts debated whether prediction markets could become a "better approach to saying everything will be financialized" compared to memecoins, noting that prediction markets offer fairer mechanics without bundling and sniping risks that plague token launches.

**Adoption Signals:**
Major crypto influencers are being signed as sponsored creators (like the Solana memecoin trader signed to Kalshi), suggesting platforms are investing heavily in distribution outside traditional crypto circles.

> "This stuff is interesting to me... it feels a bit more like fair compared to memecoins, which a lot of people used to make this argument that meme coins are fair—and like you and I both know that meme coins aren't fair."

### 3. The $70K Hyperliquid NFT Lesson: Pay Attention to Team Reputation

Hyperliquid's airdrop included an option: receive only $HYPE tokens OR receive $HYPE tokens + a Hypurrr NFT. Approximately 20-30% of recipients chose "tokens only," assuming they'd receive more tokens. They were wrong—the NFT floor price quickly hit 1,500-1,700 $HYPE (~$70,000), and those who declined received the same token allocation as everyone else.

**The Deeper Pattern:**
The hosts emphasized that Hyperliquid has consistently proven to be transparent and straightforward: "The way that the Hyperliquid team have acted previously... I feel like they just say what they mean... They're not doing something and they won't do it or we're doing this and this is exactly what they're going to get."

This contrasts with many protocols that deliberately obfuscate airdrop mechanics. The lesson: team reputation and historical behavior matter when making split-second airdrop decisions.

> "Clearly like not clearly, but it just if you're in the moment and you're thinking maybe you get fewer tokens by taking the NFT, it could be a logical choice... But it's probably a good lesson for people."

### 4. Narrative Rotation Away from Memecoins Is Real—But Cyclical

Pump.fun creator rewards and overall launchpad volumes are hitting recent lows, down to ~$1M in daily category revenue from $2-3M in prior months. The hosts noted that crypto attention is fundamentally cyclical, rotating between memecoins, perp DEXs, stablecoins, and prediction markets.

**The Framework:**
"Crypto's fundamentally an attention game and we all know this. If attention is moving away from meme coins and towards prediction markets and perstexes, that doesn't mean that memes are completely gone... but it also doesn't mean that you want to constantly stay long memes and launchpads."

Historical patterns show memecoin activity experiences regular dips (February-March, late June-July, late August) before rebounding. The question is whether this represents another cyclical low or a more sustained shift.

## Actionable Takeaways

1. **Monitor Plasma's Incentive Sustainability**: Track whether TVL remains stable as emissions potentially decrease. Watch for transitions from liquidity mining to organic product adoption through Plasma One and DEX integrations.

2. **Explore Prediction Market Opportunities Early**: Cultural and pop culture betting markets are still nascent. Consider positioning in platforms like Polymarket and Kalshi before mainstream consumer adoption accelerates.

3. **Always Take the NFT in Ambiguous Airdrops**: When team reputation is strong and wording is unclear, default to taking all available assets. The marginal upside (potential $70K) far outweighs assumed downside risk.

4. **Prepare for Narrative Rotations**: Don't fight cyclical trends. When memecoin volumes decline and perp/stablecoin narratives heat up, rotate capital accordingly rather than being "the last Japanese soldier on the island."

## Conclusion

The current market environment showcases crypto's perpetual narrative churn. Plasma demonstrates how aggressive incentives can bootstrap billions in TVL overnight—but sustainability remains uncertain. Prediction markets are quietly building consumer traction outside crypto-native users, potentially becoming the "hyper-financialization" endgame without memecoin exploitation risks.

Meanwhile, the Hyperliquid NFT incident serves as a masterclass in reading team signals and reputation when information is incomplete. As launchpad volumes decline and attention shifts elsewhere, successful traders will be those who recognize these rotations early, position accordingly, and avoid anchoring to narratives past their prime.

The lesson is clear: in crypto, what's hot today may be forgotten tomorrow—and what's forgotten today may explode next quarter. The edge lies not in predicting which narrative wins permanently, but in reading the cycles and rotating capital ahead of the crowd.
